AP Chemistry Course Exam Format

  • Section I: Multiple Choice (MCQ)
    • 60 questions
    • 90 minutes
    • 50% of exam score
  • Section II: Free Response (FRQ)
    • 7 questions (3 long, 4 short)
    • 105 minutes
    • 50% of exam score

Multiple Choice Section In-Depth

  • Questions are discrete or in sets based on stimulus (graphs, charts, passages).
  • No calculators allowed in this section.
  • Focus on conceptual understanding, application, and some memorization.
  • Answer choices may be numerical, chemical, or conceptual.
  • Guessing is not penalized, so answer every question.

Free Response Section In-Depth

  • 3 long FRQs: require detailed explanations, calculations, and multi-part answers.
  • 4 short FRQs: focus on specific topics, calculations, or lab/data analysis.
  • Calculators are allowed throughout FRQ section.
  • Show all work for calculations; partial credit may be awarded.
  • Units and significant figures matter; always label answers.

Exam Day Information

  • Bring #2 pencils for MCQ, black/blue pens for FRQ.
  • Approved calculator for FRQ section (check College Board list).
  • Government-issued or school photo ID.
  • Arrive early; doors may close before exam begins.
  • Know your AP number or student ID.
  • No electronic devices allowed during exam (phones, smartwatches, etc.).

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How is the exam scored? Both sections are weighted equally (50% each); raw scores are converted to AP scores (1-5).
  • Can I use a calculator during the MCQ? No, calculators are only allowed in the FRQ section.
  • Are partial credits awarded? Yes, especially in FRQs if you show your work.
  • What topics should I focus on? All units in the AP Chemistry curriculum; review released exams for trends.
  • Should I guess if I don't know an answer? Yes, there is no penalty for guessing.
